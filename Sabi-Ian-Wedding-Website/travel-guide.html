How to get to Brașov

The easiest and quickest way to get to Brașov is to fly directly in. Currently the only airline flying in is Wizzair, offering direct flights from a few key European cities, including Munich, Milan, Rome and London. From there it's a quick Uber or bus ride into the city.

Alternatively, you can fly into Bucharest Henri Coandă (OTP) Airport. From there, Brașov is around 2.5–3 hours away and easy to reach by train or private transfer. The train is a popular and affordable option, with regular services from the airport or from Bucharest North (Gara de Nord) straight to Brașov. Renting a car or booking a private transfer is also straightforward, especially if you're travelling in a group or with luggage.

Getting around the city

Brașov is a well-connected city, so we would recommend booking a taxi or taking the bus rather than using a car. Parking spots are notoriously difficult to find, especially in the old part of town. That said, if you do decide to rent a car, we recommend downloading the TPARK app, which lets you pay for parking straight from your phone.

For taxis, you can find both Uber and Bolt. There are also city taxis, but to use them you would either need to call a taxi company for a ride or head to a taxi rank — so we'd recommend sticking with the app-based options.

If you'd prefer to keep costs low, buses will be your best friend. Each bus ride is 5 RON (under £1), and you pay contactless on the bus.

What to See in Brașov

Brașov is a beautiful city in Romania, nestled in the heart of Transylvania and surrounded by the Carpathian Mountains. It's known for its charming medieval atmosphere, rich history, and stunning landscapes. Most of the top sightseeing spots are nestled in the Old Town:

  • Council Square (Piața Sfatului) — The picturesque, lively square in the heart of the old town, surrounded by colourful baroque buildings and charming cafes. Here you can visit the Council's House and the Museum of Urban Civilisation.
  • Black Church (Biserica Neagră) — A Gothic-style church dating back to the 14th century. One of Romania's most important landmarks, famous for its size, architecture, and the 4,000-pipe organ. Named after the smoke damage it sustained during a fire in the 17th century. Tickets ~£4.
  • Rope Street (Strada Sforii) — One of the narrowest streets in Europe, just wide enough for one person to pass through at a time.
  • Weaver's Bastion (Bastionul Țesătorilor) — One of the most interesting and well-preserved structures of the Brașov Fortress system. Entrance just over £1.
  • Catherine's Gate & The Schei Gate — The 16th and 18th century medieval gates, main entrances to Brașov's old town.
  • Schei Quarter & First Romanian School — A historical district known for its traditional Romanian houses and cobbled streets. The first school to teach solely in Romanian, founded in the 16th century.
  • The Medieval Fortifications — Includes the old fortification wall, White Tower, Graft Bastion and Black Tower.
  • Tampa Mountain — Home of the Hollywood-style "Brașov" sign. Take the cable car (~£5 one way, £7.50 return) or hike (1 hour each way). Stop for a meal at the Panoramic Restaurant — book a window table for dinner/sunset in advance!

What to See Around Brașov

  • Bran Castle (Dracula's Castle) — A medieval fortress with a fascinating history near Brașov. Famous worldwide because of the Dracula legend (though the connection is more myth than fact). Picturesque with great views of the surrounding forests.
  • Peleș Castle — Located in Sinaia, this 19th-century royal castle is considered one of the most beautiful in Europe. Built for King Carol I, known for its ornate architecture and stunning mountain views.
  • Poiana Brașov — A famous ski resort just outside Brașov, with lovely hiking trails in the summer.
  • Cantacuzino Castle — A stunning early 20th-century castle with beautiful grounds.
  • Bear Reservation — A sanctuary for brown bears rescued from captivity.
  • Făgăraș Citadel — A well-preserved 14th-century fortress in Făgăraș, now housing a museum.

Sightseeing Romania

If you're making a longer trip of it, Romania has so much more to offer:

  • Sibiu + Open Air Museum — A city with strong Saxon influence; colourful buildings, a beautiful Grand Square, and the ASTRA Museum — one of the largest open-air museums in Europe.
  • Sighișoara — A UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the best-preserved medieval towns in Europe. Birthplace of Vlad the Impaler, inspiration for Dracula.
  • The Salt Mine of Turda — A historic salt mine transformed into an underground amusement park, complete with a lake, ferris wheel, and mini-golf.
  • The Transfăgărășan Highway — One of the most scenic drives in the world, winding through the Făgăraș Mountains past Bâlea Lake and Vidraru Dam.
  • The Black Sea Coast — Resorts like Mamaia, Constanța, and Vama Veche. Constanța is the oldest continuously inhabited city in Romania, with excellent Roman ruins.
  • Danube Delta — A UNESCO World Heritage site and one of Europe's most biodiverse regions, perfect for birdwatchers and nature lovers.
  • The Painted Monasteries of Northern Moldova — UNESCO-listed monasteries famous for their exquisite exterior frescoes depicting religious themes. Most famous are Voroneț, Sucevița, Moldovița, and Humor.
